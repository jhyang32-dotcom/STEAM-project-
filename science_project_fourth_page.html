At the beginning of the experiment, neither of the radish seeds showed any signs of growth. However, after some time, both seeds sprouted at almost the same time. As the days passed, roots began to grow downward while shoots grew upward in both conditions. Small leaves appeared, and both plants continued to grow steadily with light and water. Since the radish plants under UV light and natural sunlight showed similar growth patterns and sprouted at the same time, it suggests that both light sources were able to support the early stages of plant growth.
