Conclusion

UV vs Sunlight

At the beginning of our experiment on how UV light and sunlight affect plant growth, neither plant showed any signs of sprouting. This made us feel discouraged, and we initially thought that the experiment had failed. However, after some time, the plants finally began to grow. When comparing the effects of UV light and natural sunlight, we observed that both plants started growing at almost the same time. From this, we concluded that both types of light can support plant growth in a similar way.

What went well

Firstly, the plant grew well under the given conditions, showing that the environment was suitable. At the beginning of the experiment, neither plant showed any signs of sprouting, which made us think the experiment had failed. However, after some time, both plants began to grow. When comparing UV light and natural sunlight, we observed that both plants started growing at almost the same time. This suggests that both types of light can support plant growth. The equipment was properly set up, providing consistent light exposure and making the experiment easier to manage.

What didn't went well

However, there were also some limitations in our experiment. Although the plant grew well overall, the UV light equipment was not always perfectly controlled, which may have caused inconsistent light exposure at times. In addition, the positioning of the equipment, while generally effective, was not ideal in every aspect, making it difficult to ensure that all parts of the plant received equal light. There were also some minor difficulties in maintaining stable environmental conditions, such as temperature and humidity, which could have influenced the growth of the plant. Furthermore, small errors in measurement and observation may have affected the accuracy of our results. These limitations suggest that the experiment could be improved with more precise control and better equipment arrangement in future trials.
